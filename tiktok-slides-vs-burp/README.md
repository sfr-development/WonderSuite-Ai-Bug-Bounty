## Why this works

- **Specific numbers.** "$475/yr" and "$7,125" are checkable facts. Vibes don't go viral; receipts do.
- **One real differentiator.** MCP / native AI integration is something Burp genuinely doesn't have. Lead with truth, not just price.
- **Visual VS table.** Side-by-side comparison with check/cross marks is the most shared format in security TikTok and r/netsec.
- **No insults.** The deck never says "Burp is bad." It says: "for 2026 prices, you'd expect 2026 software." Comparative, not defamatory.

## Disclaimer (cover-your-ass)

The "Burp Suite Professional $475/year" figure is PortSwigger's current public retail price for a single user license (subject to change). RAM and binary-size numbers for Burp are typical observed values from public reviews, not vendor-published. Replace with your own measurements before claiming them in court.

## Posting tips

- **Hook on slide 1.** TikTok kills you in 1.5 seconds. The "$475 for a tool your AI can't use" line is the trap.
- **Slide 2 (receipt) is the share trigger.** Concrete cost-of-ownership numbers get reposted by team leads.
- **Caption.** Something like: *"I just saved my team $7,125. Comments below tell me Burp does X — I'm waiting."*
- **Hashtags:** `#bugbounty #pentesting #infosec #burpsuite #cybersecurity #opensource #rust #ai #mcp`
- **Comments strategy.** Burp loyalists will engage. Don't argue feature-by-feature; pin a comment with the GitHub link and let the repo do the talking.
